Section 2: Property Documents & Agreements

Required Documents

Do you have these documents ready to provide to the buyer?

Why these are needed:

Buyers have a legal right to see these documents before signing a contract. They show what's officially registered about your property and any restrictions or rights that come with it.

Where to get them:

  • Title search: Order from Queensland Titles Office online or through your solicitor
  • Survey plan: Usually comes with title search, or from Titles Office

Cost: Around $30-50 total for both documents

Property Agreements & Restrictions

Are there any ongoing agreements or arrangements that will affect the new owner?

What we're looking for:

Any deals, agreements, or arrangements you've made that the new owner will need to know about or continue.

Examples:

  • Tenant living in the property
  • Neighbor using part of your driveway
  • Agreement to maintain shared fence
  • Someone else's pipes/cables crossing your land
  • Informal arrangements about property use

What type of agreements exist?

Current Tenants

Written Agreements

Verbal/Handshake Agreements

Even informal "handshake deals" can be legally binding. Examples:

  • "You can park in my driveway"
  • "I'll maintain the shared garden bed"
  • "You can access your shed through my property"

Government/Utility Rights

Common examples:

  • Power lines crossing your property
  • Water/sewer pipes under your land
  • Council drainage easements
  • NBN/telecommunications equipment

Check for: Easements listed on your title search or council notices you've received

Rental History

Has this property been rented out in the last 12 months?

Why this matters:

Queensland law says rent can only be increased once every 12 months. If you recently increased rent, the new owner can't increase it again until the 12 months is up.

Section 3: Planning, Environment & Land Use

Property Zoning

Do you know what your property is zoned for?

What is zoning?

Zoning determines what you can legally do with your property - like run a business, build extensions, or use it for short-term rentals (Airbnb).

Common zoning types:

  • Residential (houses, units)
  • Commercial (shops, offices)
  • Industrial (factories, warehouses)
  • Mixed Use (combination)
  • Rural (farming, large blocks)

Where to find it:

  • Council rates notice
  • Council website property search
  • Planning certificate from council
  • Ask your solicitor

Examples: Low Density Residential, Neighbourhood Centre, General Industrial

Government Transport & Infrastructure

Has the government contacted you about transport projects affecting your property?

What we're looking for:

Any official notices about roads, railways, or other transport infrastructure that could affect your property.

Examples:

  • New railway lines
  • Bus stops or transport hubs
  • Cycle paths or walkways
  • Airport expansion

Types of notices:

  • Letters from Main Roads Queensland
  • Council correspondence about road projects

Heritage Listings

Is your property heritage-listed or in a heritage area?

Heritage protection means:

  • Restrictions on renovations or changes

  • Potential grants or incentives available

Types of heritage listing:

What You Are NOT Required to Investigate

You do NOT need to research or provide information about these topics:

Flooding & Natural Hazards

You are not required to research flood history, natural disaster risks, or climate hazards. Only declare if you have personal experience of flooding/damage on your property.

Planning Restrictions

You are not required to research council building restrictions, development potential, business use rules, or short-term rental regulations. Only declare what you know from personal experience.

Vegetation & Wildlife

You are not required to research vegetation clearing rules, koala habitat restrictions, or protected species requirements. Only declare known restrictions or notices you have received.

Section 4: Buildings & Structures

⚠️ Declaration Requirements

You must declare known building issues, work history, and safety compliance. Only disclose what you actually know - you are not required to investigate or warrant the property.

Swimming Pool Safety

Does your property have a swimming pool or spa?

You must declare if you have:

  • In-ground or above-ground pools
  • Spas and hot tubs
  • Inflatable pools that hold more than 300mm of water
  • Any body of water capable of drowning

Your obligations:

Declare what pool safety documentation you have. You are not required to obtain certificates if you don't already have them.

Building Work History

Have you done any building work yourself in the last 6 years?

You must declare if you did:

  • Building work yourself as the main contractor
  • Major renovations or extensions
  • Structural alterations
  • Any work where you held the owner-builder permit

Your disclosure obligation:

Owner-builder work in the last 6 years must be disclosed. Provide details of what work was done and when.

Note: If you did owner-builder work, you may need to provide a Section 47 notice to the buyer.

Government Notices & Orders

Have you received any notices from authorities about building issues?

You must declare if you received:

  • Council notices about building violations
  • Show cause notices for unapproved work
  • Enforcement notices for safety issues
  • Court orders requiring building work
  • Fire safety compliance notices

Your obligation:

Disclose all notices received about your property, regardless of current status. Include copies of the notices and explain what action has been taken.

Your Asbestos Obligations ⚠️

What you must do:

  • Declare the age of your building (approximate is fine)
  • You are NOT required to test for asbestos
  • You are NOT required to warrant asbestos presence/absence
  • Only declare if you have actual knowledge of asbestos

What you don't need to do:

  • Arrange professional testing
  • Provide asbestos reports
  • Investigate potential asbestos locations
  • Make warranties about asbestos presence
